# LLM Council — Debt Battle-Test (2026-06-11)

**Question:** Does borrowing HKD 705k at a verified 1% fee (OCBC 60-day interest-free; repay within 60 days; miss it → 12-mo loan + 3% + up to 48% p.a.) to fund a ≤60-day convex AI-infra trade (MU/MRVL) make sense? User's claim: *"It's only 1%, the risk/reward makes sense."*

## Advisor summaries
- **Contrarian:** 1% prices the loan, not the trade. No native stop → −10% is a hope; MU/MRVL gap through it on earnings. Target and stop same magnitude = coin flip with a fee. 60-day cliff nails exits shut. Real cost = the HKD 77.6k pre-authorized loss. Test: "would you do this with cash and no stop?"
- **First Principles:** Auditing the wrong number. Cheap money makes a bad trade *faster*. What's the edge? If repeatable, compound own capital; if not, leverage rents a bigger mistake.
- **Expansionist (dissent):** 1% = premium on a quarter of near-free capital; 20:1 at +20%. A repeatable ~6% annualized uncollateralized facility. Concentrate; amplify via defined-risk call spreads inside day 60.
- **Executor:** FX is the killer — two manual conversions + T+1 settlement ⇒ real close ≈ **T+52**. No-stop ⇒ trade MUST be long options. Don't draw until Mac + FX dry-run proven.
- **Outsider:** "Only 1%" = the entry ticket, not the bet. Needing the asset up *on a schedule* to repay debt = a margin call with a calendar. 1% is cheap because the bank knows who usually loses.

## Consolidated review — what ALL FIVE missed
1. **Why full notional?** A HKD 200–300k position = same thesis, fraction of cliff risk; loan needn't be fully drawn.
2. **Usable window ≈ T+52, not 60.** From a drawdown now, **MRVL (~late Aug) and MU (~late Sept) earnings fall OUTSIDE** — convexity premise collapses if the thesis is those prints.
3. **Options near earnings cost 8–15% IV** → real cost ≫ 1%.
4. **"Ringfenced salary repays regardless" disguises a trading loss as a savings drawdown** — hidden, not contained.
5. **USD/HKD peg ⇒ FX risk negligible.**
6. **The plan's own checklist already encodes most objections ⇒ the failure mode is ENFORCEMENT/DISCIPLINE, not design.** → codify params for the bot to enforce.

## Verdict
**Can make sense only if restructured:** defined-risk options, partial notional (~30–40%), a real catalyst inside T+52, machine-enforced rules. As literally framed (full 705k, levered shares, −10% mental stop, out-of-window earnings catalysts) → **no.**

**Do first:** Confirm MU/MRVL catalyst dates against a T+52 close. No in-window catalyst ⇒ no edge ⇒ don't draw.
